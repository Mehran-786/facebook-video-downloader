FAQ Section

Q1. Is it free and legal to download Facebook videos?

Our tool is 100% free and works in any browser. Copyright laws differ by country, so avoid using downloaded content for commercial purposes unless you have the creator’s permission.

Q2. Do I need to install an app or software?

Not at all. It’s browser-based and runs on Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and others — on both desktop and mobile.

Q3. How do I download Facebook videos on mobile?

Copy the video link (Share → Copy Link), paste it into the field, pick your preferred quality (MP4 or MP3), and tap Download. If the “Copy Link” option doesn’t appear, open the post in a new tab to get the URL.

Q4. Can I download in Full HD (1080p or 4K)?

Absolutely. The downloader grabs the highest quality available from the original upload, which can be 1080p or even 4K. Larger files naturally take a bit longer.

Q5. Can I download Facebook Reels, Stories, or Live videos?

Public reels and stories follow the same process. Live videos become downloadable only after the broadcast finishes and the post is made public.

Q6. Can I download private Facebook videos?

Only if you can already view the video in your browser. Our tool doesn’t bypass Facebook’s privacy settings.

Q7. Will the downloaded video have a watermark?

We don’t add any watermarks. You get the original file as uploaded. If a creator added their own branding, it stays — but we leave the video untouched.

Q8. Can I convert a Facebook video to MP3?

Yes. Choose the MP3 option after the link is processed, and the audio track will be extracted, compatible with virtually any media player.

Q9. Where do downloaded videos get saved?

On mobile, they usually land in the Downloads folder or Photos/Gallery app. On desktop, they go to your browser’s default download location.

Q10. Is my data safe? Why do some videos fail to download?

We never ask for login or collect personal data — only the URL is processed. If a download fails, check that the link is correct and the video isn’t private or deleted. Regional access restrictions can occasionally cause trouble.
